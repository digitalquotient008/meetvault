## The Fundamental Difference: Marketplace vs. Your Booking System

This is the core split between the two tools.

**Booksy** is a marketplace. When your client opens the Booksy app to book with you, they see your profile — and they also see every other barber near them. That&apos;s Booksy&apos;s business model: they&apos;re a discovery platform, like Yelp for barbers. To sustain that, they need to keep users on the Booksy platform. Your clients are part of that ecosystem.

Booksy needs your clients&apos; loyalty to Booksy — not necessarily to you. When a client opens Booksy to book with you, the app actively surfaces other barbers nearby in the same session — that&apos;s not a side effect of the platform, it&apos;s how their discovery model works.

**MeetVault** is your dedicated booking system. When someone opens your MeetVault booking link, they see you. Not you and ten nearby competitors. Just your calendar, your services, your prices. The booking page carries your brand, not MeetVault&apos;s.

You own the booking relationship. MeetVault is invisible.

This difference sounds conceptual, but it shows up in real, concrete ways — especially when it comes to deposits.

## The Deposit Problem: Why Booksy&apos;s Flow Loses Clients

This is where the gap becomes concrete — and where most barbers switching to MeetVault first feel the difference.

Here&apos;s what happens when a client tries to pay a deposit through Booksy:

1. Client finds your Booksy profile
2. Client selects a service
3. Booksy prompts them to **create a Booksy account** (or log in to one they already have)
4. Client enters email, creates password, verifies account
5. Client adds payment method
6. Client finishes booking and pays deposit

That&apos;s five steps with a mandatory account creation before the deposit can be processed.

Here&apos;s what the research shows: barbers report that clients abandon this flow constantly. The interruption at step 3 — &ldquo;create an account to continue&rdquo; — is where half of attempted bookings fall apart. Clients who would have paid a deposit without hesitation hit that prompt, get annoyed, close the app, and say &ldquo;I&apos;ll do it later.&rdquo; A lot of them don&apos;t do it later.

The phrase that resonates with barbers who&apos;ve experienced this: **abandoned bookings**. Not &ldquo;deposit abandonment rates&rdquo; — abandoned bookings. The client wanted to come in. The friction in the booking system turned them away. You lost an appointment to a software problem.

**MeetVault&apos;s deposit flow works like this:**

1. Client opens your booking link
2. Client selects service, picks a date
3. Client enters name, email, and card
4. Deposit is collected. Appointment confirmed.

No account creation. No Booksy login. No extra steps. The deposit is automatic at checkout — clients book and pay in one flow without creating an account anywhere.

If a client ghosts or cancels late, the deposit is already in your account. You don&apos;t chase it because you already have it.

One of the most important — and least talked about — differences between these two platforms comes down to a simple question: **who can see your clients?**

| | MeetVault | Booksy |
|---|---|---|
| **Who can see your client list?** | Only you | Anyone on the Booksy marketplace |
| **Your clients see competitor barbers?** | No | Yes — Booksy shows nearby alternatives *in the same session they&apos;re booking with you* |
| **Booksy can recommend your clients to another barber?** | N/A | Yes, it&apos;s their business model |
| **Your booking page is branded?** | Your brand, your colors, your link | Your Booksy profile — not your brand |

On Booksy, every client you build a relationship with is also a Booksy user. Booksy can show them other barbers nearby. Booksy can recommend alternative spots based on availability. That&apos;s not a bug — it&apos;s how their discovery model works. They need users engaged on the platform to make their marketplace valuable to barbers who are paying for visibility.

The business model tension here is real: Booksy gets paid by barbers to attract clients, and they retain clients by keeping them inside Booksy&apos;s ecosystem. Your clients&apos; loyalty to Booksy can, in some cases, compete with their loyalty to you.

MeetVault doesn&apos;t have a marketplace. There&apos;s nothing for your clients to browse. Your booking link goes to your page — and that&apos;s the whole experience.
